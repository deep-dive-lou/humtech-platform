What

A statistical process control chart for the {{ chart.label }} rate. The shaded bands show 1, 2, and 3 standard deviations from the centre.

Control limits

Centre (average rate): {{ "%.2f" | format(chart.center * 100) }}%. UCL: {{ "%.2f" | format(chart.ucl * 100) }}%. LCL: {{ "%.2f" | format(chart.lcl * 100) }}%. Points outside these bounds are almost certainly signals, not noise.

What to look for

Orange diamond markers indicate Western Electric rule violations — non-random patterns that suggest the process has shifted. Check the CUSUM below for sustained drift. If all points stay within the shaded bands with no patterns, the metric is stable.

CUSUM

{{ info_icon('cusum_' + loop.index|string) }}
What CUSUM+ and CUSUM- show

CUSUM+ (darker blue) accumulates positive deviations — rising means the rate has been consistently above target. CUSUM- (lighter blue) accumulates negative deviations. If either crosses the signal threshold, a drift is confirmed.

Why this complements the P-chart

A P-chart catches sudden spikes. CUSUM catches gradual drift — e.g. a metric slowly falling 0.2% per month for 5 months. Each individual month looks fine on the P-chart; CUSUM accumulates the signal and fires an alert. Together, they cover both types of shift.
